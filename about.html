Biography

Chinyere Gloria Mbaka is a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Rochester Institute of Technology and a Graduate Research Assistant in the Respiratory Technologies Laboratory.

Her work sits at the intersection of embedded systems, sensing, exposure science, and computational modeling. She is particularly interested in how device characteristics, human behavior, and real-world usage patterns shape exposure outcomes, and she applies data-driven methods to better understand inhalant product use and its downstream effects.

Her research interests include embedded sensing, puff topography monitoring, ambulatory measurement, behavior-based yield modeling, pharmacokinetic modeling, and machine learning for health-related data analysis.

Before beginning her doctoral studies, she served as a Lieutenant Commander in the Nigerian Navy, where she held technical and leadership roles involving electrical, electronic, and computer systems. That experience continues to inform her approach to research, combining technical rigor with practical problem-solving and multidisciplinary collaboration.
