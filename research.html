Research Overview

My research examines how product characteristics, use behavior, and exposure dynamics influence downstream exposure biomarkers, particularly nicotine and cotinine response.

Research Areas

  • Embedded systems
  • Ambulatory sensing
  • Puff topography monitoring
  • Exposure science
  • Behavior-based yield modeling
  • Pharmacokinetic modeling
  • Signal processing
  • Machine learning

Current Projects

  • Development and evaluation of Wireless Personal Use Monitors (wPUMs)
  • Natural-environment observation studies
  • Behavior-linked exposure and biomarker response analysis
  • Emissions testing and product characterization

Selected Outputs

  1. Ambulatory Puff Topography of Participants in a Weeklong Natural Environment Observation Study
  2. Hyde Brand Electronic Cigarette Aerosol Emissions Characterization
  3. Assessing Monitors for Characterizing the Natural Environment Puff Topography of Tobacco Product Users
