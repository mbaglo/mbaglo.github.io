# About

## Biography
I am a Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Rochester Institute of Technology and a Graduate Research Assistant in the Respiratory Technology Lab. 

My academic and professional interests include embedded systems, ambulatory sensing, puff topography monitoring, exposure science, and computational modeling—particularly how hardware, software, and measurement intersect to solve real-world health problems.

## Academic Interests
- Embedded systems and sensor integration
- Wearable and ambulatory monitoring technologies
- Puff topography and inhalant behavior analysis
- Exposure science and health-related measurement systems
- Computational and pharmacokinetic modeling
- Research communication and technical documentation

## Current Focus
I am developing and evaluating sensing systems and analytical methods to characterize exposures, behavior, and outcomes in ambulatory environments.

Through this website, I share research progress, technical work, and reflections from doctoral study.
